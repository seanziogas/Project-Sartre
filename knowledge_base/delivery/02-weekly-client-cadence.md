# Weekly Client Cadence

## Overview

Every active engagement follows a consistent weekly rhythm. This cadence is not rigid — it adapts to the client's pace and the engagement's current phase — but the structure exists for a reason: predictability builds trust. Clients should never have to wonder what's happening.

The GTME owns this cadence. The MD is not in the day-to-day flow.

---

## The Standard Weekly Structure

### Monday — Async Update (GTME or TOS)
**What it is:** A Slack message to the client's external channel covering what was done last week, what's in progress, and any blockers or questions.

**Who sends it:** The GTME. Senior/great TOS members may draft and send these independently.

**Format:**
```
👋 Weekly update — [Client Name]

✅ Last week
- [What was completed]
- [What was shipped or reviewed]

🔨 This week
- [What's in progress]
- [What we're targeting for completion]

🔴 Blockers / open items
- [Anything the client needs to act on]
- [Any access or data still outstanding]
```

Keep it tight. Two or three bullets per section is usually enough. The goal is visibility, not a status report novel.

---

### Weekly Call (GTME-led)
**Cadence:** Once per week, 30–60 minutes. Standing time confirmed at kickoff.

**Who attends:**
- GTME (always)
- MD (Month 1 of all new engagements; thereafter as needed)
- TOS (rarely — only if client complexity warrants it)
- Client: day-to-day contact (always), economic buyer or other stakeholders (as relevant)

**Agenda structure:**

1. **Wins / what shipped** — Start with what got done. Lead with value. Don't open with blockers.
2. **What's in progress** — Current build status; where things stand vs. the roadmap.
3. **Blockers and open items** — What the pod needs from the client. Be specific. Don't let this drag.
4. **Next week** — What they can expect by the next call.
5. **Open floor** — Any questions, feedback, or direction from the client.

**What makes a good weekly call:**
- The GTME prepares. They know what's happening on every active track and can speak to it clearly.
- The call starts and ends on time.
- The GTME drives the agenda — they don't wait for the client to lead.
- Decisions are made, not deferred. If the client needs to choose between two approaches, bring both with a recommendation.
- The GTME takes notes during the call and captures action items.

**What to avoid:**
- Opening with a long recap the client already received in Slack
- Raising problems without a proposed solution
- Letting the call run 45 minutes over because the agenda wasn't scoped
- Leaving without confirmed next steps for both sides

---

### Wednesday — Mid-Week Async (GTME or TOS)
**What it is:** A mid-week Slack update following the same format as Monday. Covers what's moved since Monday, anything in progress, and any blockers.

**Who sends it:** GTME. Senior/great TOS members may draft and send these independently.

This is a fixed requirement every week, not situational. Same structure as Monday.

---

### Friday — End-of-Week Async (GTME or TOS)
**What it is:** End-of-week Slack update. Same format as Monday and Wednesday — what got done this week, what carries into next week, any outstanding items.

**Who sends it:** GTME. Senior/great TOS members may draft and send these independently.

---

## Monday.com as a Communication Layer

Monday.com is the client's always-on view of progress. The GTME keeps it updated continuously — not just before calls.

Every active track should have:
- Tasks broken into discrete, completable units
- Status updated as work moves (Not Started → In Progress → Done)
- Due dates that reflect realistic delivery timelines
- Brief status updates on any task that's blocked or behind

If a client checks Monday.com between calls, they should never be surprised. The board is a source of truth, not a post-call update.

---

## How Cadence Shifts by Phase

| Phase | Call Frequency | Async Frequency | MD Involvement |
|---|---|---|---|
| Activation | Weekly | Mon / Wed / Fri | High — MD attends all calls |
| Building | Weekly | Mon / Wed / Fri | Low — weekly 1:1 with GTME only |

---

## Post-Call Follow-Up

Within 3 hours of every weekly call, the GTME sends a follow-up in Slack or email covering:
- Key decisions made
- Action items (both sides, with owners and deadlines)
- Any questions raised that need async resolution

This is not optional. If it happened on the call and isn't in writing, it didn't happen.

---

## Key Principle

**The client should never have to ask for an update.** If they're asking, the cadence has already failed. Proactive, consistent communication is the standard — not the exception.
