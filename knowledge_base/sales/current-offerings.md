# Current Offerings — The Kiln

A reference for reps and new hires on how we structure engagements, what we build, and what clients actually get. We are a fractional GTM engineering agency — more execution than consulting. We build, we document, we hand off.

## Two Engagement Types

---

### 1. GTM Engineering Engagement (Standard)

Track-based execution. We build systems, automate workflows, and hand off working infrastructure. The primary offering. Most of our work starts from Clay but may branch out to tools like SFDC, n8n, Smartlead/Instantly, SEPs and other larts of the GTM stack.

**Duration:** 3–6 months. Most run 3–4 months. Larger scopes run 5–6 months usually for larger enterprises or extremely fast growing companies (Harvey/Crescendo) that need a lot of hands

**Pricing:** $18,000–$25,000/month.

**Pod:** GTM Engineer (12 hrs/wk) + Technical Ops Specialist (12 hrs/wk) + Managing Director (3-4 hrs/wk). Every client gets at least one pod.

**Cadence:** Weekly syncs. Daily updates. Full documentation, SOPs, and handoff package at close.

**What the client owns:** Strategy, messaging direction, copy tone.

**What we don't do:** Consulting decks without execution. One-off audits. Tool procurement. We build things that run.

### 2. GTM Transformation Engagement (Consulting + Strategy) WIP

For 2X portfolio companies, PE-backed businesses, and late adopters who need to understand what they have before they can responsibly add anything new. Same monthly investment, different work — more conversation, less code.

*Start with the funnel, not the tools.*

Before touching a single AI vendor or building a single workflow, we map the entire customer journey end to end. Every step from anonymous traffic to closed-won to expansion. What exists, what's broken, what's redundant, what's missing. Most companies find it clarifying and embarrassing at the same time: 15 steps between a form fill and a personalized email that should be 5. Five tools that can do IP de-anonymization when one would do. Integrated tools with unused features sitting idle.

Mapping the existing motion honestly is step one — because it becomes the focusing mechanism for everything that follows.

**Duration:** 2–3 months. Structured as a series of workshops, ride-alongs, and documentation sprints before any build work begins. Can transition into a standard GTM Engineering engagement.

**Pricing:** Same as standard ($18,000–$20,000/month). Less work being built = more time with the client. More calls per week, more depth per conversation.

**Format:**
- 2–3 calls per week (vs. 1 weekly sync in a standard engagement)
- Ride-alongs with reps and ops team to observe current workflows firsthand
- Lifecycle mapping workshops — every step from anonymous traffic to closed-won to expansion documented
- Tech stack scoring (see below)
- No build work in phase one. The output is a map and a roadmap, not a system.

**Phase 1: Map the Funnel**

Before touching a single AI vendor, we map the entire customer journey. Every step, every handoff, every tool involved. Anonymous traffic → individual identified → meeting booked → opportunity → closed-won → expansion. What we typically find is embarrassing and clarifying at the same time: 15 steps between a form fill and a personalized email that should be 5. Redundant tools solving the same problem. Features paid for but never turned on. Integrations that were supposed to work but don't.

Mapping the existing motion honestly is step one — because it becomes the focusing mechanism for everything that follows. You can't responsibly add anything new until you understand what you already have.

**Phase 2: Score the Stack**

Once the funnel is mapped, we run a green/yellow/red assessment across every major vendor. Three criteria:

- **Is this best-in-class for what we need it to do?**
- **How completely have we implemented it?**
- **Is it architected for the AI-centered, dynamic GTM workflows we're building toward?**

The results are usually uncomfortable. Most spend lands in yellow and red — including vendors the company has built its GTM motion around for years. Many leading, well-known tools are fundamentally not architected for dynamic personalization and automation at the level modern GTM requires. The output isn't a vendor takedown — it's a clear picture of where to invest further, where to consolidate, and where to minimize workflow dependency going forward.

**Who this is for:**
- 2X portfolio companies and PE-backed businesses where the mandate is clear but the starting point isn't
- Late adopters who've been told to "do something with AI" but don't have a reliable picture of their current motion
- Companies that have accumulated tools over years without ever honestly assessing whether they're actually working
- Organizations where change management is as important as the technology

**What they get at the end of phase one:**
- Full funnel map — every step, every tool, every handoff, every gap
- Green/yellow/red tech stack scorecard with consolidation recommendations
- Clear picture of what to build on, what to minimize, and what to replace ahead of renewal
- Consolidation and cost savings case — meaningful to a PE-backed board
- A rolling 12-month buy/build/consolidate plan for the tech stack
- Prioritized roadmap of GTM engineering interventions, ordered by ROI and dependency
- Clear brief for a standard GTM Engineering engagement if they choose to move forward

The honest assessment does two things simultaneously: it gives leadership a clear view of consolidation and cost savings opportunities — which matters to any PE-backed board — and it clearly prioritizes which platforms to double down on versus which to start replacing ahead of renewal. Most companies don't have this picture. The ones that do make better, faster decisions on every subsequent technology investment.

**Phase 3: Narrow to a Short List With a Hard ROI Filter**

After mapping the funnel and scoring the stack, we poll RevOps and GTM stakeholders on how AI should be applied. This generates a long list — often 40–50 ideas. That's expected and useful. The ideas aren't the problem. The problem is moving without a filter.

We run the list through three cuts:

1. **Funnel leakage filter** — which ideas directly address the gaps identified in Phase 1? This typically gets the list from 50 to ~15.
2. **5x ROI hurdle** — every remaining initiative must be tied to a specific, measurable point in the funnel with a pressure-tested improvement target. This is what gets exec and board buy-in. The 15 become 6.
3. **Effort vs. impact ranking** — of the ideas that clear the ROI hurdle, which ones are high-leverage enough to move the needle materially, and which ones are fast enough to build in the first engagement?

**The north star metric for every initiative: Cost per Dollar Booked.**

Every initiative that makes the shortlist should show how it moves this number. Saving reps time is nice, but time savings are unrealized gains — they only matter when they translate into more output per rep or fewer reps for the same output. Cost savings are fine, but nobody enjoys cost-controlling their way to EBITDA growth. The language that gets exec and board buy-in is revenue acceleration: more pipeline, higher conversion rates, shorter sales cycles.

**Metrics worth attaching to each initiative:**

Every idea on the shortlist should map to at least one of these:

- **Top-of-funnel volume** — more accounts identified, more contacts sourced, more coverage of the TAM
- **Funnel conversion rate** — MQL to SQL, SQL to opportunity, opportunity to close
- **Cross-sell / expansion pipeline** — net new revenue from existing customers
- **Speed to lead** — time from signal or form fill to first rep touch
- **Rep productivity** — revenue-generating activities per day, pipeline per rep, time saved on research and admin
- **Market coverage** — percentage of the TAM identified, enriched, and activated
- **CAC efficiency** — pipeline generated per dollar of GTM spend (including ad spend, tool spend, and headcount)
- **Data quality** — contact coverage %, field completion %, enrichment accuracy (upstream; everything else depends on it)

The initiatives that survive this filter are the ones that go into Phase 4. Everything else goes on the roadmap for later — not abandoned, just sequenced.

**Phase 4: Data Foundation**

See Phase 3 section below — this is where execution begins.

### Phase 4 — Data Foundation

You cannot layer automation on top of a broken foundation. This is the first execution phase — and the prerequisite for everything that follows.

Before we touch Clay or write a single enrichment workflow, we run a discovery process to understand what the foundation actually needs to be:

**What are the fields, and why do they matter?**
We interview reps, ops, and leadership to find out what information they actually use to qualify, prioritize, and personalize. Not what's in the CRM today — what they wish was there. What does a rep check before a call? What would make a T1 account obvious at a glance? What data point would change how they approach an outreach?

This surfaces both standard enrichment needs (industry, revenue, employee count, tech stack, funding) and non-standard ones — the fields that are specific to this company's ICP and motion. Custom signals. Niche data that doesn't come from a standard provider. Job title classifications that require interpretation. Facility or location-level data. Compliance, certification, or regulatory indicators. Whatever the rep's judgment currently relies on — we systematize it.

**What are the signals, and why do they matter?**
Same exercise for signals. What trigger events indicate a company is in-market, expanding, or worth reaching out to right now? Job postings? Leadership changes? Funding rounds? Competitor activity? Product usage indicators? We map these explicitly — what to track, where to find it, and what action it should trigger.

**Then we build the foundation:**
- TAM mapping — sourcing every account in the market, categorized and imported into the CRM
- CRM enrichment — standard and non-standard fields populated and maintained on a refresh cadence
- Scoring model — ICP fit score, segment tier, and signal score built from the field and signal discovery above
- CRM hygiene — deduplication, normalization, field rework where needed
- Salesforce or HubSpot structural cleanup — field architecture, object relationships, and data model aligned to the motion we're building

**Where tools like Kernel or a Clay-led CRM rework come in:**
Some companies need structural CRM surgery before enrichment is useful — Salesforce fields that don't match the ICP, object models that weren't built for modern GTM, or years of dirty data sitting underneath. This is where Kernel (for Salesforce dedup and hierarchy management) or a Clay-led field remapping can be necessary before anything else will work reliably.

The output of Phase 4 is a CRM that reps trust, a scoring model they can act on, and a signal layer they can see — so that every automation built on top of it has a clean, accurate foundation to run from.

## GTM Engineering Tracks (EXAMPLE Standard Engagement)

Every engagement starts with kickoff, then moves through tracks matched to the client's priorities. Data foundation comes first — everything else depends on it.

---

### Track 0 — Kickoff & Activation (Weeks 1–2)

Always first.

- Tool access setup (Clay, CRM, enrichment providers, sequencers)
- Environment audit — what's connected, what's broken, what's missing
- ICP definition and field mapping confirmed
- Signal priority list agreed
- Order of operations locked
- Week 1 quick win identified

---

### Track A — Data Foundation & CRM Enrichment

The most common starting point. Everything downstream depends on clean data.

**What we build:**
- Account enrichment: firmographics, technographics, funding history, industry classification, custom fields
- Contact discovery and enrichment: titles, emails, LinkedIn, direct dials
- Deduplication and normalization
- Evergreen refresh workflows — enrichment runs on cadence, not one-time
- CRM sync (Salesforce or HubSpot) with field mapping and write-back

**Real examples:**
- *Novata* — enriched full account universe with ESG-relevant firmographics and contact discovery across target personas
- *Vitable Health* — built CRM enrichment foundation from scratch, normalized 3 years of fragmented data
- *CohnReznick* — replaced $130K+ in data subscriptions (ZoomInfo, Sales Intel, Hoover's, PitchBook) with a single evergreen Clay enrichment layer
- *Optimizely* — enriched 240K+ account universe, fixed segment misclassification, resolved dupe hierarchies

---

### Track B — TAM Mapping & Account Scoring

For clients without a complete picture of their market, or whose scoring model is broken or missing.

**What we build:**
- Full TAM import — sourcing from Clay, LinkedIn Sales Nav, Apollo, Cognism, and custom scrapers (Firecrawl, Zenrows, Python) depending on the market
- ICP scoring model with weighted criteria — fit score, segment tier, signal score
- Account tiering (T1/T2/T3) written into CRM
- Custom classification for hard-to-find data (non-standard titles, certifications, facility size, geographic coverage)

**Real examples:**
- *Codes Health* — national law firm TAM from court records, NPI/CMS, and web scraping. No standard database covered the niche.
- *Waldo Capital* — sourced 25K+ companies, enriched with funding history and founding team signals, built VC deal sourcing scoring model
- *Sequoia* — portfolio company contact database, founder network mapping, event-matched audience targeting
- *Intertek* — sourced niche food safety accounts by facility type, certification status, and sub-vertical signal

---

### Track C — Signal Infrastructure & Intelligence Layer

Builds the detection logic that tells you when an account or contact is worth reaching out to — and routes that signal to the right person or system.

**What we build:**
- Job change detection — contacts moving in or out of target accounts
- Hiring signal monitoring — job postings indicating budget, initiative, or pain
- Funding and M&A signal detection
- Intent signal stacking — 6sense, G2, web visit, behavioral signals into a composite score
- Competitor signal monitoring — new customers, G2 reviews, LinkedIn engagement
- Signal-to-CRM routing — writes back to records, triggers notifications or tasks

**Real examples:**
- *Rillet* — LinkedIn engagement intelligence + competitor targeting via job postings and review signals
- *SDG* — farming signal system for expansion opportunities in existing accounts; guided selling alerts to reps
- *Drata* — signal stacking across job change, funding, hiring, and intent — routed to Gong Engage as rep-facing alerts
- *Narvar* — signal detection for existing customers approaching renewal or showing expansion indicators

---

### Track D — Inbound Enrichment & Routing

High-ROI, fast to build. Often the first quick win.

**What we build:**
- Real-time enrichment on form fills — firmographics, ICP fit score, contact data appended at entry
- Lead scoring and routing — right rep, sequence, or nurture flow based on fit and intent
- Speed-to-lead automation — high-fit leads get immediate notification or auto-enrollment
- Dedup and normalization before CRM entry
- Integration with Salesforce, HubSpot, Outreach, Gong Engage

**Real examples:**
- *Optimizely* — inbound triage across 8 product segments, EMEA and North America; replaced 5+ hours/week of manual lead routing
- *Reco AI* — full inbound qualification and routing overhaul; enrichment + scoring + sequence enrollment automated end-to-end
- *SDG* — inbound optimization across multiple entry points; routing logic by segment and territory

---

### Track E — Outbound Automation & GTM Co-Pilot

The execution layer. Signal fires → account identified → contact sourced → brief built → message drafted → rep reviews or system sends. Two modes depending on the play and the client's preference.

**Fully Automated Outbound**
For high-volume, signal-triggered plays where speed matters more than rep review. We handle the full infrastructure layer:
- Inbox setup and domain configuration — dedicated sending domains, DNS records, DMARC/DKIM/SPF
- Deliverability optimization — warmup schedules, sending limits, bounce and spam signal monitoring
- Signal-triggered sequence enrollment — account sourced, contact enriched, copy generated, sent automatically
- Tools: SmartLead, Instantly (email), Heyreach (LinkedIn)

**Human-in-the-Loop Outbound**
For enterprise targets, higher-touch plays, or any account where rep judgment adds value before the message goes out. The system does everything upstream — research, enrichment, copy generation, signal surfacing — and pushes pre-loaded, ready-to-review tasks into the rep's existing tools. One click to approve and send.
- Tools: Salesloft, Outreach, Gong Engage
- Rep wakes up to a queue of signal-triggered, pre-researched, pre-written outreach tasks
- No context switching, no manual research, no blank page
- The rep adds judgment; the system handles everything else

**Real examples:**
- *Rillet* — event outreach automation (webinar/dinner invites segmented by deal stage), competitor displacement sequences, LinkedIn plays
- *Waldo Capital V2* — agentic sourcing engine with scoring, signal detection, and automated outreach to M&A targets
- *Codes Health* — event automation for legal industry events; contact sourcing + enrichment + invite sequencing end-to-end
- *Narvar* — programmatic messaging pilot with AI-drafted copy pushed into rep review queue; approved with one click; scaled to team rollout in month 3

---

### Track F — Systems Integration & Reverse ETL

For clients with data in a warehouse or fragmented system that isn't accessible where reps work.

**What we build:**
- Reverse ETL from Snowflake, BigQuery into Salesforce/HubSpot
- Intent and product usage data surfaced in CRM from internal sources
- Third-party API integrations where off-the-shelf connectors don't exist
- Unified data layer connecting signals from multiple sources into a single account view

**Real examples:**
- *Optimizely* — connected Salesforce with internal data warehouse to unify product usage signals with outbound targeting
- *Responsive* — integration layer connecting CRM with proposal data to enable expansion signal detection

---

### Track G — Rep Workflow & Daily Prep Automation

For clients where the primary problem is rep efficiency — too much time on research and admin, not enough time in front of buyers.

**What we build:**
- Pre-call brief automation — account summary, recent signals, contact history, talking points before every call
- Daily prioritization views — tiered account lists with context, refreshed automatically
- Account research automation — enrichment, news, job postings, LinkedIn, internal CRM data in a single brief
- Delivered via Slack or CRM, wherever reps work

**Real examples:**
- *Drata* — GTM co-pilot on Gong Engage; signal-triggered rep alerts with context and suggested next action
- *SDG* — guided selling alerts; reps notified when farming signals fire with context on what changed and what to do
