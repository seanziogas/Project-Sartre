# The Kiln — ICP Definition

**One-line ICP:** Mid-market B2B SaaS companies (or SaaS-like orgs) that have invested in a modern GTM stack, understand what AI-assisted selling looks like, and have hit the execution ceiling — they know what needs to be done but don't have the hands or expertise to build it fast.

---

## Score Boosters (Apply Across All Tiers)

These signals lift a company's fit score regardless of tier placement:

- **PE sponsor mandate:** PE-backed company whose sponsor (Apax, Maritech, Insight Partners, Kleiner Perkins) is actively pushing an AI/GTM transformation agenda — creates urgency and unlocks budget without requiring internal selling
- **Known PE portco:** Company is a portfolio company of Apax, Maritech, Insight Partners, or Kleiner Perkins — relationship leverage compresses the sales cycle and warms the intro
- **Post-funding event:** Series B closed within last 6 months — board pressure to show pipeline growth, budget freshly allocated, CRO/VP Sales typically new to the role

---

## Tier 1 — Core ICP (80–100 fit)

The deal closes fastest here. Champion is motivated, budget exists, and they already understand what they're buying.

### Company Profile
- **Industry:** B2B SaaS (primarily), or SaaS-like GTM motion (subscription revenue, AE/SDR-driven sales)
- **Size:** 100–2,000 employees; $10M–$500M ARR
- **Funding:** Series B or later; or PE-backed with sponsor mandate
- **AI/GTM maturity:** Already using Clay, Salesforce/HubSpot, intent data, or some combination — has the stack but not the connective tissue
- **Execution gap:** Understands the work required, has tried to build internally, ran into capacity or capability ceiling

### Champion Profile
- **Title:** VP Sales, Head of Sales Ops, SDR Director/Manager, RevOps leader
- **Situation:** 6–18 months into the role, needs a win to establish credibility
- **Dynamic:** Has exec air cover (CRO or CEO has approved the initiative) but is not the economic buyer
- **Motivation:** Wants to 2x pipeline without headcount, or automate what the team is doing manually today

### Buying Triggers
- Board pressure to show pipeline growth without BDR headcount increase
- New VP/CRO hired and building a modern GTM motion from scratch
- PE sponsor mandating AI adoption across portfolio
- 60–80% of CRM untouched / no systematic outbound motion
- SDRs spending majority of time on research rather than conversations

### Examples
- Reco AI — Series B, cybersecurity SaaS, board-pressured, champion VP Sales
- Crescendo — AI-native, understands the work, high execution ceiling
- Harvey — same profile, top of tier
- Notion, Canva — larger but same maturity/execution gap pattern
- Optimizely — $400M ARR, PE-backed (Insight Partners), SDR Ops champion
- Rillet — smaller org, shorter engagement (6–10 weeks), same Tier 1 profile

## Tier 2 — Stretch ICP (60–79 fit)

Has a champion who wants to move but the organization hasn't arrived yet. Sellable, but requires more internal selling from the champion and more patience from us.

### Company Profile
- **Industry:** B2B SaaS or non-tech companies with a SaaS-like sales motion
- **Size:** 50–500 employees
- **Funding:** Series A–B; or mid-market without PE sponsorship
- **AI/GTM maturity:** Limited — champion is ahead of the org. May not have Clay or formal GTM tooling. Champion is the sophistication, not the company.
- **Execution gap:** Champion sees the opportunity but is fighting internal inertia (legacy process, skeptical leadership)

### Champion Profile
- Same profile as Tier 1 — but the exec air cover is softer or still being built
- Often a newer person coming in to "shake things up"
- May not have budget approved yet — part of the sale is helping the champion get internal buy-in

### Buying Triggers
- New Sales Ops/RevOps hire inheriting a broken system and wants to modernize
- Company coming off a bad quarter and leadership is open to trying something new
- PE sponsor mandate is present but not yet cascaded down to the champion

### Examples
- RJ Young — champion-led, org not fully there
- AdRoll — has the profile but no strong champion identified yet; needs a trigger event (reorg, new hire, missed quota) to activate
- Modal, Profound — AI-aware culture, shorter engagements, champion-led but smaller org scope

## Tier 3 — Watch List (40–59 fit)

High potential but long cycles, organizational complexity, or budget constraints make these slow-burn opportunities.

### Profile
- Large-scale transformation underway (hundreds of employees, multi-department GTM)
- PE or private equity firms themselves (not portcos) — see PE section below
- Seed-stage companies where budget ceiling limits deal size (price sensitivity risk is real)
- Companies that haven't invested in GTM tooling yet and need education before they can buy

### What to Watch For
- Series B raise → re-score as Tier 1
- New CRO/VP Sales hire → champion trigger, re-evaluate
- PE sponsor relationship activates → score boost into Tier 1

### Examples
- PE firms themselves (Waldo Capital, Unity Partners, Insight Partners as a firm) — viable but slower
- Codes Health — seed stage, CEO price-anchoring to DIY cost; revisit post-Series A

## PE Segment — Firms Themselves (Tier 3)

PE firms are a distinct buyer segment. Long sales cycles, diffuse stakeholders, and the work is fundamentally different from selling to a SaaS portco. But the upside is structural: close one relationship with an operating partner or VP of Portfolio Ops and it unlocks introductions across the entire portfolio.

### The Core Problem We Solve for PE Firms

Operating partners at PE firms face a version of the same problem every portfolio CRO faces — but at scale. Every portco CRO under pressure to "invest in AI GTM" means something different by it:

- Some mean **cost reduction:** fewer SDRs, automated sequences, leaner headcount
- Some mean **data enrichment:** better signals, cleaner CRM, less manual research
- Some mean **full-stack transformation:** ICP scoring, signal-based outreach, real-time market intelligence flowing into every rep's workflow

These aren't just different definitions — they're different starting points, ROI stories, and implementation paths. The portco CRO pushed toward "AI GTM" without that clarity will do what they always do under pressure: pilot something, stall, and move on. The operating partner gets blamed for a failed initiative.

The Kiln's value to a PE firm is helping operating partners get specific before the portco even starts spending. The firms making real progress start with one question: *what problem, in what business, by what metric, by when?*

### What We Sell to PE Firms

| Engagement Type | What It Is |
|----------------|-----------|
| **Portfolio ICP + TAM build** | Define the actual ICP from closed-won deal data, build a dynamic scored TAM (350K+ accounts → 30K Tier 1 targets). What used to take 3 months and a team of data engineers now takes under a week. Becomes the standard playbook at every portco close. |
| **AI GTM diagnostic** | Rapid assessment across portcos: where does each company actually fall on the AI GTM maturity spectrum? What's the right intervention at each stage? Gives the operating partner a portfolio-wide view. |
| **GTM infrastructure build** | For portcos that need it: inbound enrichment, CRM cleanup, signal layer, outbound engine. Same work as Tier 1/Tier 2 portco engagements — just sourced through the PE relationship. |
| **M&A sourcing infra** | For the firm itself: AI scoring model for deal sourcing, CRM enrichment of target companies, signal-based tracking of acquisition targets. (Waldo Capital, Unity Partners pattern.) |

### Who We Sell To at PE Firms
- **Operating Partners (GTM/Revenue)** — primary champion; owns the GTM mandate across portfolio
- **VP Portfolio Operations** — manages cross-portco initiatives; wants scalable playbooks
- **Managing Director / GP** — economic buyer; typically brought in after operating partner validates the ROI

### Score Logic
- PE firm itself → Tier 3 (relationship asset, not near-term pipeline)
- PE firm with active GTM mandate and engaged operating partner → Tier 2 (can move faster)
- PE portco of a known relationship firm (Apax, Maritech, Insight, Kleiner) → Tier 1 score boost (treat as warm intro, compress the sales cycle)

## Disqualification Criteria

Walk away or deprioritize if:

- **Seed stage with no funding event imminent** — budget ceiling doesn't support $20K/month; CEO is anchoring on DIY cost
- **No internal champion** — CEO is the only buyer and is skeptical; no one internally advocating for the work
- **No GTM tooling investment** — hasn't bought Clay, intent data, or structured CRM; needs too much education before they can buy, and will under-appreciate what's being built
- **Non-recurring revenue model** — project-based or transactional businesses; GTM motion doesn't translate
- **Champion without exec air cover** — wants to buy but can't get it approved; deal stalls indefinitely without a trigger event

*For the "we'll handle this internally" objection, see [agency-vs-inhouse.md](agency-vs-inhouse.md).*

## Universal Buying Triggers

These apply across all tiers — if one of these is true, prioritize the account regardless of tier:

| Trigger | Why It Matters |
|---------|---------------|
| Board-mandated pipeline growth with hiring freeze | Budget exists, urgency is real, ROI frame is clear |
| PE sponsor pushing AI transformation | Unlocks budget, shortens cycle, reduces skepticism |
| New VP Sales / CRO hired in last 6 months | Building their motion from scratch, wants to show impact fast |
| Series B closed in last 6 months | Fresh budget allocation, board expectations being set |
| Known portco of Apax / Maritech / Insight / Kleiner | Relationship leverage changes the entire dynamic |
| 60%+ of CRM records untouched | Operational pain is visible, easy ROI case |
| SDRs spending majority of time on non-selling tasks | Efficiency case is obvious and measurable |
