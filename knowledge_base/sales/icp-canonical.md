# Ideal Customer Profile (ICP)

## Who We Sell To

The Kiln targets **B2B companies with $100M+ in revenue** that have a mature go-to-market motion — fully built-out marketing and sales teams who are looking to improve operational efficiency and scale without adding headcount.

We are **not** a fit for early-stage startups or companies still figuring out their GTM motion. Our model works best when the client already has process, people, and tooling in place and wants to engineer their way to better output.

---

## Firmographic Profile

| Attribute | Target |
|---|---|
| **Revenue** | $100M+ ARR |
| **Company Stage** | Series C+ or established enterprise |
| **GTM Motion** | Outbound-driven, inbound-assisted, or full-cycle sales |
| **Team** | Dedicated Sales, Marketing, and/or RevOps function |
| **CRM** | Active CRM in use (Salesforce, HubSpot, or Attio) |
| **Clay** | Using Clay, or open to adopting it |

---

## Persona Profile

### Primary Buyer
- **Title:** VP of Sales, VP of Revenue Operations, VP of Marketing, CRO
- **Motivation:** Improve GTM efficiency without headcount; scale what's working; fix what's broken
- **Pain:** Reps spending time on non-selling tasks; CRM is unreliable; outbound is manual and low-quality; inbound leads aren't being worked properly
- **Success metric:** More pipeline, better data, faster reps, cleaner CRM

### Champion (often different from buyer)
- **Title:** RevOps Manager/Director, Sales Ops Lead, Marketing Ops Lead
- **Motivation:** Get something built that they've wanted for a while but don't have bandwidth or Clay expertise to build themselves
- **Pain:** Too many tools, too many manual processes, no one to actually build the solution
- **Success metric:** The system works, their team uses it, they look good internally

---

## What Makes a Great Fit

A prospect is a strong fit when they have:

1. **An active Clay presence** — either already using Clay (any plan) or explicitly interested in adopting it as the core data orchestration layer
2. **A concrete problem, not a vague goal** — "We need to enrich and dedupe our HubSpot before Q3 planning" beats "We want to improve our GTM"
3. **Internal access and willingness to collaborate** — they'll give us CRM access, introduce us to RevOps and ICs, and engage actively in the activation phase
4. **Realistic expectations on timeline** — understands that building takes weeks, not days, and that we need a ramp period
5. **Budget allocated** — not "exploring," not "considering next quarter." The budget conversation should be done before they talk to delivery.

---

## Red Flags / Poor Fit Signals

| Signal | Why It's a Problem |
|---|---|
| Revenue under $50M or pre-PMF | GTM motion isn't mature enough; too much volatility |
| No CRM or CRM not in use | Can't enrich or route what isn't tracked |
| Wants "strategy only" — no execution | Our model is build-and-operate, not consulting |
| Expecting full delivery in Week 1 | Activation phase exists for a reason; impatience kills engagements |
| Single stakeholder with no internal buy-in | If the champion leaves, the engagement collapses |
| "We just need Clay templates" | Not worth the retainer; they need Clay training, not us |

---

## Vertical Concentration

The Kiln is industry-agnostic but has strong depth in:
- **SaaS / B2B Tech** — majority of current client base
- **Financial Services / Fintech** — growing segment
- **Professional Services** — some presence

We avoid heavily regulated industries where data handling is complex (healthcare, insurance with strict PHI/PII requirements) unless there's a specific RevOps use case that doesn't touch protected data.
