# Sales Process

## Overview

The Kiln's sales process runs from first contact to signed SOW and deal close. Sales owns this process end-to-end. The delivery team (MD + GTME + TOS) does not engage until after the deal closes and the handoff document is complete.

---

## Stages

### Stage 1: Outbound / Inbound Lead
- Lead identified via outbound campaign, referral, inbound form, or conference/event
- Initial qualification against ICP (revenue, GTM maturity, Clay presence or appetite)
- Non-qualified leads are disqualified here — do not pass to discovery

### Stage 2: Discovery Call
- Goal: understand the prospect's pain, goals, and fit
- Cover: company background, GTM motion, current toolstack, biggest friction points, what "success" looks like
- Outcome: either a clear problem that The Kiln can solve, or a disqualification
- Follow-up: brief email recapping key points and proposed next step

### Stage 3: Solution Design
- Based on discovery, sales drafts a proposed engagement structure (tracks, scope, timeline)
- Often involves a second call or async collaboration to refine the approach
- Key outputs:
  - Which of The Kiln's four core use cases applies
  - Proposed track structure (what we'll build and in what order)
  - Estimated engagement duration

### Stage 4: Proposal & SOW
- Sales drafts the Statement of Work (SOW)
- SOW includes: engagement type, tracks, deliverables, pricing, timeline, terms
- Proposal is reviewed by MD before it goes to the prospect
- SOW is sent for review → negotiation if needed → countersign

### Stage 5: Deal Close
- Contract signed
- Sales completes the Handoff Document (see `delivery/01_sales_handoff_to_kickoff.md`)
- Internal handoff call is scheduled with the pod
- CRM is updated with all deal data and notes

---

## Key Principles

**Sales does not overpromise.** Any timeline, deliverable, or capability commitment made in the sales process becomes a constraint for delivery. If it's said on a sales call, the GTME will be held to it.

**Delivery doesn't close deals.** GTMEs and TOSs should not be in sales calls or proposal conversations. Their job begins at the internal handoff.

**Handoff document is non-negotiable.** Sales does not hand off a deal without a completed handoff doc. The quality of the handoff directly affects the quality of the first month.

## Handoff Checklist (Sales → Delivery)

Before closing out of a deal, sales must confirm:

- [ ] Handoff document fully completed
- [ ] Fathom recordings linked in handoff doc
- [ ] SOW linked in handoff doc
- [ ] Internal handoff call scheduled with MD + GTME + TOS
- [ ] Monday board created (or flagged for pod to create)
- [ ] Internal Slack channel created
- [ ] External (client-facing) Slack channel created
- [ ] Renewal check-in date set (~1 month before engagement end)

See `delivery/01_sales_handoff_to_kickoff.md` for the full handoff process.
